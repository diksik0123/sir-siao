Timeline

  • 1975

    Born on February 17, 1975, in Buffalo, New York.

  • 1990s

    Early childhood marked by abuse and a difficult home environment. He struggles with obesity and low self-esteem. Enlists in the United States Air Force but fails pararescue training due to health issues.

  • 1999

    Joins the U.S. Navy and successfully completes SEAL training on his third attempt, graduating with BUD/S Class 235.

  • 2001-2005

    Serves in Iraq and Afghanistan as a Navy SEAL. Undertakes various missions and earns numerous accolades.

  • 2005

    Loses several friends in the Operation Red Wings mission, which deeply affects him and drives him to honor their memory through endurance challenges.

  • 2006

    Begins participating in ultramarathons and other extreme endurance events. Completes the San Diego One Day 24-hour ultramarathon, running 100 miles in under 19 hours with little prior training.

  • 2007

    Competes in the Badwater 135, one of the toughest ultramarathons, finishing 5th overall.

  • 2008-2013

    Sets the world record for the most pull-ups in 24 hours in 2013, completing 4,030 pull-ups.

  • 2015

    Retires from the U.S. Navy after 20 years of service.

  • 2016-Present

    Releases his memoir "Can't Hurt Me: Master Your Mind and Defy the Odds" in 2018, which becomes a bestseller. Becomes a sought-after motivational speaker, sharing his story and inspiring others to overcome adversity.

  • 2019-Present

    Continues to participate in endurance races and ultramarathons. Gains a large following on social media, inspiring millions with his message of mental toughness and resilience.
